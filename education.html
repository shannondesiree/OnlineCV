PDA Software Development SCQF Level 6 - PASS

Glasgow Clyde College, 2023

With the equivalency of an Advance Higher, the 12-week PDA involved different aspects that I hadn't yet come across in my online studies to explore computer science - including foreign computer languages like Java and extensive planning principles relating to software development lifecycles and project management. The course covered general programming principles, algorithms, and data handling and came with a focus on the Agile methodologies vs the Waterfall SDLC model using Java as the primary programming language. CS50: Intro to Computer Science

Harvard Online via EdX, 2022

After exploring my options to begin my journey to becoming a software developer, I identified the CS50 online 10-week course from Harvard via EdX as a wise and worthy first step for beginners into the vast world of computer science. I realised this was the career choice for me and that this could provide what I was looking for from a career after various encounters with likeminded people and some industry research. The time I took to reflect on my own character and where and what I would excel in was my leading motivation to undertake this specific course and I have been climbing the mountain of ever since. The course introduced programming in a clear and understandable way, building a foundation with the machine-friendly language of C and expaining its evolution to the higher level programming languages such as Python, HTML and JavaScript. I learned about search/sort algorithms and concepts like recursion, debugging, arrays, memory, conditoinal statements, data types, operators and loops. The course also touched on PHP and databases, SQL, CSS and Cyber Security. The syllabus required the submission of over 30 coding assignements and a final project where I used JavaScript with HTML and CSS to create a point-of-sale system as seen via my Github profile.

PGDE Secondary Biology with Science - PASS

Univeristy of the West of Scotland, 2020

I undertook the Post Graduate Diploma in Education, qualifying with a PASS in Secondary Biology Teaching with Science in 2020. The course duration was one year and involved part class attendance at University and part placement on school where I undertook 3 separate, full-time 2, 5 and 6 week placements respectively at Louden Academy and Saint Andrews RC Academy. This is where I initially began perfecting my planning and presenting skills as an educator and over the course as part of the Univeristy Diploma, I carried out real-time research into pupil confidence and the poverty-related attainment gap and used my findings to contribute towards dissertaions which, combined with my performance on placement, contributed to my formal overall assessment of the program. I developed an aptitude for different hardware used to deliver course content to pupils like SmartBoards and the software tools that went with them, mastering my competency in Microsoft Excel, PowerPoint, Office and Word. My experience was particularly adverse due to the coronavirus pandemic where staff at my placement establishment faced the difficulties of distant and online teaching for the first time. In University, I learned about the History of teaching, the General Teaching Council for Scotland (GTCS) and the Scottish Qualifications Authority (SQA). After obtaining the PGDE, students are allocated to a school to complete their initial teaching year as an Newly Qualified Teacher (NQT) where they work from provisional teacher registration to full teacher registration and my initial teacher training probationary year was at Penicuik High School in Midlothian where, after an academic year, I gained full registration teacher status. The course nature proved incredibly demanding and challenged me in many ways including my confidence in presentations and in speaking and my awareness of implementing a variety of pedagogical techniques to embed learning.

BSc Applied Bioscience and Zoology (sandwich Degree) - PASS

University of the West of Scotland, 2015

As an animal lover, I really enjoyed learning more about life and the natural world in this Degree. In year 1, my grades for the mathematics modules where A in Dealing with Data, B in Mathematics of Space and Change and C in Sequences and Patterns, gaining 60 MATH credits in total. In these years, I gained a further 40 BIOL credits through the Biodiversity and Cells and Molecules modules as well as 20 CHEM credits in I was awarded in the Scientific Investigation where I graded B1. In year 2, I achieved a futher 100 BIOL credits in the Introductory Microbiology, Genetics, Biosphere and Man, Human Biology, Vertebrate Physiology and Practical Skills in Biomedicine and Environmental Health modules. In Year 3, I achieved a further 140 BIOL credits from the Genetics, Work-Related Learning, Animal Diversity, Bio-Professional Practice, Biological Conservation, Animal Behaviour and Entomology and Parasitology modules. The course included a short but hands-on excursion to Millport where students boarded a trawler boat, collected marine wildlife and tested on their ability to identify and research a wide variety living species. The program also allowed for animal-related work experience as part of the Work-Related Learning module where I chose to volunteer at a seal rescue centre in Ireland for 10 days.
