Voluntary Work

In 2015 I completed five months of zoological-based voluntary work in Asia with three animal charities. I began with working as a panda keeper at the Giant Panda centre in Ya'an City, China - CCRPGP - the same centre where Edinburgh Zoo's Tian Tian and Yuan Gaun were transferred from. With pandas being more threatening than they look, the work here mainly consisted of cutting bamboo, preparing fruit cake, cleaning enlcosures and helping with enlcosure maintenaince. At the end, we were lucky to be invited to spend some hands-on time with some of the panda cubs which was extremely rewarding. I arrived to Phnom Tamao Wildlife Centre in Cambodia where I volunteered with the Free the Bears Sanctuary working with Malaysian sun and Asiatic black bears. Coincidently, it was this centre that sourced the sun bears also residing in Edinburgh Zoo! The work here was more strenuous and involved hammock-weaving, building enrichment tasks, cleaning and feeding. I had the opporunity to observe click trainers with some of the bears, visit the bear nursury that housed the cubs and work with many of the resuced bears with amputated limbs who had been saved from the fate of old traditional medicines like bear paw soup - a cruel method many people still believe to harbour natural healing powers. I then travelled to Thailand where I volunteered for WFFT (Wildlife Friends Foundation Thailand) in Chandg-mai where I gained husbandry experience for Asian elephants. I went out to banana tree fields and harvested the trunks for the elephants. We made banana balls, hosed the animals and even walked some of the older residents.

I learned so much about the maintenance and around the clock care needed for these animals as well as knowledge of the species themselves, local importance, taxonomy, conservational status, and the usually sad historical background as to why they are now so rarely found in the wild. I adore the work these charities do for the welfare and husbandry of these species, and as a result I am confident with pursuing my dream of dedicating my life to benefit animals. Furthermore, travelling across Asia itself broadened my mind in many other cultural ways. For 10 days during December 2014 and January 2015 I completed a placement of voluntary work-based learning at Seal Rescue Ireland. As part of one of my third year modules at university I was given the opportunity to be part of this non-profit organisation with the aim of rescuing, rehabilitating and releasing common and grey seal pups. The tasks I undertook consisted of preparing, weighing, and distributing feeds, cleaning and laundry, restraints for tube-feeds, administering medicine, and clerical work. Learning about the two species of seal and the difference between them was exciting and sometimes I was required to pass this information on to visitors coming to the centre through talks. I was glad that I chose to travel and do something different for this experience and getting so close to wild, marine mammals was truly extraordinary. In June 2013, I travelled to South Africa and Swaziland to volunteer with the charity VESA. I worked with a variety of native animals including cheetahs, servals, crocodiles and alligators, as well as working at an orphanage, a school, and on a construction site. The mixture of the three of these widened the help I provided making my experience very interesting, however it was the animal work that appealed to me most. The two weeks here lit a spark to my love for working with animals and inspired my more recent voluntary trip to Asia. Additionally, I have also completed short term volunteer work with the Scottish Owl Centre where I conducted talks and flying presentations to the public with the world’s largest collection of trained owls as well as feeding and contributing to the rearing of several owlets.
