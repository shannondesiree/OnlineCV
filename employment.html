Voluntary Work

In 2015 I completed five months of zoological-based voluntary work in Asia. The work I did here was exclusively to benefit charities that dealt with endangered animals – specifically giant pandas at CCRCGP near Ya’an city in China, Malaysian sun and Asiatic black bears at the Free the Bears centre in Phnom Tamao, Cambodia, and Asian elephants with WFFT in Thailand. Coincidently, the pandas and sun bears in Edinburgh Zoo have both come from these very centres. A combination of the climate and labour made working very challenging, but nonetheless I found the whole experience extremely rewarding. I learned so much about the maintenance and around the clock care needed for these animals as well as knowledge of the species themselves, local importance, taxonomy, conservational status, and the usually sad historical background as to why they are now so rarely found in the wild. I adore the work these charities do for the welfare and husbandry of these species, and as a result I am confident with pursuing my dream of dedicating my life to benefit animals. Furthermore, travelling across Asia itself broadened my mind in many other cultural ways. For 10 days during December 2014 and January 2015 I completed a placement of voluntary work-based learning at Seal Rescue Ireland. As part of one of my third year modules at university I was given the opportunity to be part of this non-profit organisation with the aim of rescuing, rehabilitating and releasing common and grey seal pups. The tasks I undertook consisted of preparing, weighing, and distributing feeds, cleaning and laundry, restraints for tube-feeds, administering medicine, and clerical work. Learning about the two species of seal and the difference between them was exciting and sometimes I was required to pass this information on to visitors coming to the centre through talks. I was glad that I chose to travel and do something different for this experience and getting so close to wild, marine mammals was truly extraordinary. In June 2013, I travelled to South Africa and Swaziland to volunteer with the charity VESA. I worked with a variety of native animals including cheetahs, servals, crocodiles and alligators, as well as working at an orphanage, a school, and on a construction site. The mixture of the three of these widened the help I provided making my experience very interesting, however it was the animal work that appealed to me most. The two weeks here lit a spark to my love for working with animals and inspired my more recent voluntary trip to Asia. Additionally, I have also completed short term volunteer work with the Scottish Owl Centre where I conducted talks and flying presentations to the public with the world’s largest collection of trained owls as well as feeding and contributing to the rearing of several owlets.
